Udupi Shri Krishna Temple is a well-known historic Hindu temple dedicated to Krishna and Dvaita Matha located in the city of Udupi in Karnataka, India.

Explore More of Udupi Sri Krishna Matha

The Udupi Shri Krishna Temple, located in the coastal town of Udupi in Karnataka, India, is a revered pilgrimage site dedicated to Lord Krishna. It is celebrated for its unique traditions, spiritual ambiance, and historical significance. The temple is closely associated with Madhvacharya, a 13th-century saint and philosopher who established the Dvaita school of Hindu philosophy. He is believed to have installed the idol of Lord Krishna, which remains the centerpiece of devotion.

A unique feature of the temple is the Kanakana Kindi, a small, silver-plated window through which devotees can view the deity. Legend has it that this window is named after Kanakadasa, a devout poet-saint, who was granted a divine vision of Lord Krishna through it. The temple is managed by the Ashta Mathas (eight monasteries) established by Madhvacharya, with each Matha taking turns to oversee its administration.

The temple's architecture reflects traditional South Indian style, featuring intricately carved wooden and stone structures. The temple courtyard serves as the venue for grand festivals, including the annual Chariot Festival (Rathotsava), which attracts thousands of devotees. Apart from being a spiritual center, the temple is a hub of cultural activities. It plays a significant role in promoting classical music, dance, and Sanskrit scholarship. Pilgrims and tourists are drawn not only to its spiritual offerings but also to the opportunity to explore the rich heritage of the region.

Udupi Shri Krishna Temple is well-connected by air, rail, and road. The nearest airport is Mangalore International Airport,about 60 km away, while the Udupi Railway Station and road networks make it easily accessible from major cities. A visit to this temple provides a serene spiritual experience and a glimpse into the cultural richness of Udupi.
