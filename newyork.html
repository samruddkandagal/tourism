The Perampalli Church, officially known as the Immaculate Conception Church, is a historic and serene Roman Catholic church located in Perampalli, near Udupi, Karnataka. Established in 1924, the church holds significant spiritual and cultural importance in the region, serving as a center for worship and community gatherings for nearly a century.

The church is renowned for its beautiful architecture, blending traditional design with colonial influences. Surrounded by lush greenery and peaceful surroundings, it offers a tranquil setting for prayer and reflection. The church features a striking altar, adorned with intricate designs, and stained-glass windows that create a serene ambiance. It hosts regular masses, special feasts, and events that attract devotees and visitors from across the region, particularly during the annual feast of the Immaculate Conception.

The Perampalli Church is not only a place of spiritual solace but also a landmark that reflects the rich heritage and unwavering faith of the local Catholic community. Its calm and scenic environment makes it an ideal destination for those seeking peace and a deeper connection with spirituality.
